About Me


I am an Assistant Professor of History at Smith College. Prior to Joining Smith, I served as a full-time Instructor of Imperial Chinese History at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, where I taught a wide range of undergraduate courses on Chinese history and East Asian civilization. I received my Ph.D. in East Asian Studies from the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign in 2025. I earned the Dr. Sandra J. Finley Teacher Scholar Certificate in 2024 and completed the LAS & OVCDEI Inclusive Pedagogy Certificate in 2026. In 2018, I attended the Winter School in the History and Philosophy of Science at the University of Sydney.

My research explores the intersection of law, medicine, and madness in late imperial China, with particular interests in legal medicine, and the history of the body. I am currently developing a book manuscript titled Legal Justice, Medical Authority, and Madness in China, 1600-1930.
